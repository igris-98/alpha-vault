Aida, the magic formula to creating compelling copy that sells.
Aida is an extremely powerful formula that, if followed, will ensure your content is always primed to sell.

You can use the AIDA formula in most of your promotional copy. Whether you're creating press releases, landing pages, email campaigns, or product ads.

It works in four stages:
- Attract
- Interest
- Desire
- Action

To attract your customers attention, interest them, and then make them desire what you're selling. The final stage is to make them take action, which usually means getting them to make a purchase or subscribe.

# **Attract:** 
- Attracting customers is the first and most critical step.
- There are many ways to attract customers using your content, but one important way is through your **headers**. With headlines, you need to grab people's attention from the off so they click through or keep reading on.
- Headers can be short or a little longer, but try to keep them as brief as possible while still being specific. **Getting specific is critical.** 
	- No one is going to click through if they're not sure what they're going to be reading. Time is precious, so you have to make people want to spend theirs reading your content by showing them what's in it for them.
	- One way of making your headers more specific is by including numbers in them. You won't be able to use them every time in every header you write, but you can supercharge your content by including them wherever possible.
- **Sub headers** are also crucial to help break up blocks of text and add structure.
- So use them in your content, but be sure to make them exciting and relevant. A good header is **provocative** and presses people's buttons. It can make them **excited**, **intrigued**, or even **angry**. But one thing it **should never do is be boring.**
- So don't **play it safe with your headers.** Stand out by asking leading questions or promise value by offering to give people knowledge they never had before.
- You should aim to charge your headers with **emotional words** like **amazing**, **exceptional**, **Terrible**, **Horrible**. But **don't use too many and don't go overboard.**
	- The terrible truth about most skincare products is a powerful, emotive and intriguing header as is.
	- Is your diet endangering your health?
	- Ten ways to stop Losing money and your Facebook ad spend is even better. As not only does everyone want to save money, you're giving people an incentive to click through and read your content by promising to give them a practical solution.

## An industry survey conducted by Mazda found that number headlines got 327% more click throughs than question headlines.

## Use FOMO(Fear Of Missing Out) in your headers to attract more click throughs.
- FOMO is another great principle to use when writing your headlines.
- It works by tapping into a worry or common concern people have, making them want to find out more so they can either prevent or solve the problem.
- A headline like Five Ways You Can Guarantee Your Cat Lives Longer uses the FOMO principle.
- The number one cholesterol slashing ingredient. Are you getting enough?

# **Interest:**
- Once you've got people's attention, you need to move on to the second stage of AIDA and keep their interest.
- This part of your copy is often called the lead and it is hugely important. Aim to keep your lead to a max of about **eight sentences for optimum effect**.
- One way of ensuring you keep people reading is by using **intrigue**. You can do this by teasing that you have the solution to their problem or fear **without telling them exactly what it is**. Or you can create interest by sharing a short story about someone who used your product or service and got great results.
- Don't jump in straight away and hit them with the details of your product.


# **Desire:**
- After you have attracted people with a great header and interested them enough so they want to keep reading.
-  You can't simply talk about how great your product is. You can't simply talk about how great your product is. You need to show potential customers what's in it for them. 
- The what's in it for them.
	- This principal is another staple of copywriting as customers are focused on themselves and want what they want. 
	- To demonstrate what's in it for your customers. Always outline the benefits of your product or service in any copy you create.
	- Show people how what you are offering will directly improve their lives or give them the results they're seeking.
- Never just outline your product or service features. No one really cares whether or not your product was created by the finest master craftsman on the planet. But they do care. If you can explain why this makes it 100 times more effective than any other product out there. As this gives them a reason to purchase.

# **ACTION:**
- This is the last stage of the AIDA formula and will obviously be vital to the success of your brand. If you don't make sales, you can't stay in business, right? So you need to motivate readers of your content to buy or sign up.
- Most people are lazy and they have too much choice.
- They need an **incentive** in order to take action.
- The Incentibe could be :
	- A Great Deal
	- A Limited Time Offer
	- A product and service that gives them benefits Or result they just can't get anywhere else.
